Abstract

The Plant Disease Detection project utilizes Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to identify plant diseases from images of affected leaves. The system is trained on a dataset comprising both healthy and 39 different disease-affected leaves, undergoing preprocessing techniques before being fed into the model. It enables real-time diagnosis through a mobile or web application, offering early detection and actionable recommendations to prevent crop losses and optimize fertilizer use. With each identified disease, the system suggests suitable fertilizers along with direct purchasing links, improving crop management and intervention efficiency.
