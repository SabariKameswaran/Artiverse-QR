Abstract

The Medicinal Plant Identification and Information Retrieval app uses AI to bridge the gap between people and natural remedies from plants. It employs Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) for image analysis and multiclass classification algorithms to identify plant species and therapeutic groups based on appearance. Generative AI models like Groq or Google PaLM provide real-time data on each plant’s therapeutic properties, uses in folk medicine, and side effects. The system leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) for natural language processing, enabling quick translation and personalized information. Developed with Django for backend management, the app targets healthcare enthusiasts, researchers, and farmers, promoting awareness of medicinal plants’ healing values.
